Introduction to ICS security

ICS

Generic term that encompasses several types of control systems often found in the industrial sectors and critical infrastructures.

ICSs controls physical processes

  • Electric generation, transmission and distribution
  • Petroleum refining and processing
  • Chemical processing
  • Oil and Gas pipelines
  • Mine Operation

Types of ICS

  • Supervisory Control and Data Acquisition(SCADA) Systems
  • Distributed Control Systems (DCS)
  • Programmable Logic Controllers (PLC)
Generally referred as SCADA

SCADA

A system that collects data from various collection devices that monitor and/or control something and consolidates this at a central server(s) for visualisation and historization.

DCS

A system typically used to control continuous or batch-oriented processes consisting of functionally and/or geographically distributed controllers and I/O interconnected via networks for communication and monitoring.

PLC

A microprocessor-based device used to control industrial processes or machines. They provide advanced functions, including analog monitoring, control and high speed motion control as well as share data over communication networks.

Control System Strengths and Weaknesses



Control System Strengths


High Degree of Availability
  • Redundant Control Servers
  • Redundant Historians
  • Redundant Control Center LAN
  • Diverse WAN Communication Paths
    • Frame Relay and Dial Back-Ups
  • Backup Control Centers
High Degree of Authorization
  • Commands can come from any where
  • Commands can be Automated for Emergency
  • Commands can be issued Remotely
  • High level of command Integrity
  • High level of Trust

Control System Weaknesses



Total Authorization
  • Does not require Identification and Authentication
  • Does not verify from where the command was issued
  • Does not validate from whom the command was issuesd
  • Accepts everything without question
Total Trust
  • Relies on Trusted Operators
  • Relies on Trusted Communication
  • Relies on Trusted Systems
  • Believes everything without question
Legacy
  • Not designed wit security in mind
  • Often proprietary and untested
  • Does not play well with new OSâ€™s
Increasingly Relying on Standard IT Services
  • TCP/IP and Ethernet
  • Windows and Unix Servers
  • Oracle and SQL Server Databases
  • Web Servers and Web Browser
Vulnerability to Cyber Attacks
  • Systems facing the untrusted (Internet)
  • No security in encapsulated serial
Information Availability
  • Serial comminication are well documented
  • Minor concern

Increased Threat to SCADA Systems

  • Adoption of standardized Technologies witb known vulnerabilities
  • Connectivity of control systems to other networks
  • Contraints on the use of existing security technologies and practices
  • Insecure remote connections
  • Widespread availability of technical information about control systems
