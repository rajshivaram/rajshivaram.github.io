ICS

ICS Protocols

FieldBus Protocol

  • Replaced point-to-point and parallel communication with serial communication.
  • Standardized communications
  • Used to communicate from Control Center or Master Terminal Unit (MTU) to a PLC or Remote Terminal Unit (RTU).
  • Used to communicate from PLC or RTU to actuators and sensors.

RS-232 and RS-485

RS-232

  • Traditional name for a series of standards for serial binary single-ended data and control signals connecting between DTE (data terminal equipment) and DCE(data communications equipment)
  • Current version is (TIA)-232-F, issued in 1997
  • Used to be standard for serial communications between computers until late 1990’s – superseeded by USB
  • Remains in use in the industrial sector

RS-485

  • Current version is TIA-485
  • Still widely used, no signs of decline in popularity
  • Programming is more difficult
  • Data is sent and received on the same two wires, so the timing of the controlling BUS is critical.

MODBUS

  • Developed in the late 1970s by Modicon, Inc (Now Schneider-Electric)
  • It is commonly used for connecting devices in the Industrial environment.
  • It is free and open-sourced
  • Uses client server architecture
  • Based on Polling Requests
  • Default TCP port 502

Distributed Network Protocol 3 (DNP3)

  • Westronic (now GE-Harris) developed in 1993
  • Community managed (DNP.org)
  • Used primarily in Electric and Water/Wastewater industries.
  • Tolerates EMI-induced distortion and poor transmission media
  • similar to modbus in many ways
  • Default TCP Port 20000

HART

Highway Addressable Remote Transducer (HART) Protocol

  • Master-Slave Communication
  • Two simultaneous communication channels: the 4-20mA analog signal and a digital signal
  • Managed by the FieldComm Group
  • Three classes of commands:
    • Universal
    • Common Practice
    • Device Specific

Industrial Ethernet

Basically “serial comms” encapsulated in TCP/IP packets

  • Modbus becomes Modbus TCP
  • DNP3 becomes DNP3 over TCP/IP
  • HART becomes HART-IP
  • Foundation Fieldbus H1 becomes HSE
  • DeviceNet becomes EtherNet/IP
  • ProfiBus becomes ProfiNet

TASE 2.0 and ICCP

Inter-control Center Comm Protocol (ICCP) [AKA TASE 2.]

  • Communicating between control centers or between substations on the electric grid
  • Does not provide authentication or encryption
  • Client-Server model

Common Industry Protocol (CIP)

  • Manufacturing automation applications
  • Used for control, safety, synchronization, motion, configuration and information
  • Community managed (ODVA.org)
  • EtherNet/IP TCP 44818 & UDP 2222
  • Four Protocols:
    • EtherNet/IP
    • CompoNet
    • ControlNet
    • DeviceNet

Foundation Fieldbus H1 / HSE

  • H1: serial over twisted pair
  • HSE: Encapsulation over Ethernet

BACnet

Building Automation and Control Networks

  • commonly used with existing Ethernet infrastructure, VLAN and WAN
  • Devices plug directly into Ethernet switches
  • Uses UDP/IP for compatability with existing IP infrastructure
  • BACnet/ip is peer-to-peer

PROFIBUS and PROFINET

  • Developed by ProfiBus/ProfiNet International e.V
  • Open standard for industrial automation
  • Serial or TCP/IP
  • keyword or XML
  • ProfiNET IO uses TCP/UDP ports 34962, 34963, 34964.
